Point 1. Define user needs

Develop knowledge of your users and what that means for your technology project or programme.

Read more about point 1

What behaviours have you observed in relation to Point 1?

Select all behaviours that you have seen.

Consider the following:

  • Have you carried out user research in the form of multiple interviews or observations of the users?
  • Do you have a thorough knowledge of who your users are?
  • Does your evidence support your understanding of your users’ needs?
  • Can you describe how your service/product reflects the needs of the user?

What good looks like

The team/department have carried out user research in the form of multiple interviews to find out who the user is and what they need. In order to meet all the user’s needs the project or programme will need to employ/assign people with the right range of skills and experience. The team should continue to do user research at key points throughout the project and programme to refine their deliverables and help keep the project or programme on track. At the end of the project or programme it is useful to do a user research round up of the findings in case they can be used by other teams as part of their research.
