Anti-patterns

The presence of any of the following anti-patterns might indicate an area for concern.

  • No end-users have been recruited, and requirements and requirements have been developed based only on the knowledge of the existing service delivery team.
  • Users have been polled only in group sessions during which concepts and plans are presented and feedback requested in general terms.
  • The language of requirements developed is excessively technical or system-focused and does not reflect what an ordinary user would say or think.
  • The list of assumed requirements, personas or user needs has not changed significantly from the business case stage
